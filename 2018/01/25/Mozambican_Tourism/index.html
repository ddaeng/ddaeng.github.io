What is somewhere unusual to visit, somewhere on the “off the beaten track”?

Surrounding Maputo, the capital of Mozambique, there are plenty of Portuguese-styled houses, museums and art galleries. However, some places that aren’t as famous might include:

  • The Afrin Prestige Hotel: for their artefact gallery of weaponry from the years of warfare; the war for independence and the civil war.
  • The Central Railway Station: for its long history and exquisite architecture. The station was actually designed by Gustav Effiel, however, Eiffel never stepped foot on Mozambican land.
  • Anvil Bay: just on the tip of the Machangulo peninsula, Anvil Bay is covered with huts and greenery with excellent scenery; the sea.

A simple day trip visiting these places might be:

Visas:

It is required that citizens from countries: USA, Canada, Australia, Britain and the rest of the EU, have a visa when entering Mozambique.

It is recommended that you receive your visa in advance from the Mozambique High Commission/Embassy. However, upon arrival, you may be able to obtain a visa if you live in a country with no Mozambican mission. The citizens of Botswana, Malawi, Mauritius, Swaziland, South Africa, Tanzania, Zambia and Zimbabwe do not require an entry visa when traveling to Mozambique for no more than 30 days and not for residence or working purposes.

There are two main types of visas available in Mozambique:

Single-entry visa: the time limit is from one day to one month and the visa is renewable to a maximum of 90 days.

Multiple-entry visa: the time limit is from one day to 90 days for tourism multiple-entry visas but for business multiple-entry visas, it is from one day to 180 days.

There are six categories for visas:

  • Diplomatic
  • Official
  • Courtesy
  • Tourism
  • Visit
  • Business
  • Student
  • Residence
  • Transit

Visa applicants need to submit these documents to be eligible for a visa:

  • A valid passport with at least six months validity and at least three or more blank pages.

  • Photocopy of the main passport page/s.

  • Two colour passport photos.

  • A document to confirm the applicant’s address during his/her time in Mozambique. This may be either a hotel booking confirmation or an invitation letter from the host in Mozambique, which is required if the applicant will be staying at a private home with his/her family or friends. The invitation letter/Term of Responsibility must be signed by the host and authenticated by an official authority.

  • A return ticket and adequate funds for the duration of their stay in Mozambique

  • For Business Visas, it is also required a letter of invitation from the official or business institution in Mozambique, or a Term of Responsibility from an official or business institution in the United Kingdom if travelling for private business or volunteer activities.
