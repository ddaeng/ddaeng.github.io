Urgent Issues in Mozambique

Education:

After 1974, the war of independence, few schools were available to teach people and more started to build but the civil war had destroyed many of these facilities. Now, there are more schools appearing to educate children, but that might not be enough.

In 2005, primary education was announced free, now nearly all children are attending a primary school.

However, even this being the case, there are very few qualified teachers, meaning there would be large classes and lack of proper, hygienic facilities. Children that reside in rural areas, particularly up to the north of Mozambique, are forced to walk long distances just to get to school.

Statistics - Education:

It is a tradition for girls to marry before 14 so girls put at disadvantage education-wise, so less than a third of Mozambican girls know how to read and write

In 2009, only 55% of adults were able to read and write.

What are the major health problems that affect the daily lives of Mozambicans?

The top five diseases in Mozambique are:

  1. HIV 24%
  2. Malaria 15%
  3. Lower Respiratory Infections 6%
  4. Cancer 5%
  5. Tuberculosis 5%
Statistics - Health:

There are about 600 people who die each year because of HIV/AIDS, the number only increasing as the years go by.
