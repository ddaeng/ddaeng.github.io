What types of government does Mozambique have?

After the Mozambican Civil War in 1992, the FRELIMO (Front for the Liberation of Mozambique) and RENAMO (Mozambican National Resistance) leaders, President Chissano and Afonso Dhlakama, came together in a peace treaty, signed in Rome on 4 October 1992. Now, the government of Mozambique is a republic but also a multi-party democracy as both president and prime minister work together and also there are multiple parties who are eligible to become president and prime minister.

Currently, the unicameral parliament is referred to as the Assembly of the Republic, 250 members being in the assembly and three parties: FRELIMO, RENAMO and the Democratic Movement of Mozambique. What is the currency of Mozambique?

The currency of Mozambique is the Mozambican metical, plural being meticals. The currency symbol of the Mozambican metical in MT, 1 Australian dollar being 44.39 Mozambican metical. There are 6 notes in the Mozambican currency: 20 meticais, 50 meticais, 100 meticais, 200 meticais, 500 meticais and 1000 meticais, the five coins being: 50 centavos, 1 meticai, 2 meticais, 5 meticais and 10 meticais.

What are the major natural resources found in Mozambique?

The main natural resources in Mozambique include:

  • Iron ore
  • Tantalite
  • Natural gas
  • Heavy mineral sand
  • Graphite
  • Marble
  • Sea salt
  • Diamonds
  • Uranium
  • Coal
  • Petroleum

What are the major industries in Mozambique?

Major industries in Mozambique include petroleum products, textiles, cement, glass, tobacco and beverages. In the major urban areas of Maputo, Beira and Nampula, nearly all the manufacturing factories are there.
